Public Interest Design Case Study

University of Texas School of Architecture, 2012

SUSTAINABLE DESIGN / INFORMATION DESIGN

Project Overview

This project involved the assessment of the environmental, economic, and social impacts of proxy, a public project, on the surrounding community. When an earthquake damaged San Franciscoâ€™s Central Freeway and left a series of abandoned lot in the Hayes Valley neighborhood, local residents banded together to demand temporary solutions to activate the unused lots. Envelope A+D entered the picture with their concept for a collection of retail and food vendors in temporary structures made of shipping containers called proxy. Proxy has been a critical piece in a series of initiatives that has signifcantly changed the nature of the neighborhood over the last ten years.

Research Methods

For several weeks we observed users and documented visits, interactions, and activities through photography. We also conducted interviews with various stakeholders to determine their involvement, intentions, and receptions of the project.
